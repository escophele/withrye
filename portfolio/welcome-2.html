From the moment you start using your new iPad mini 4, you’re going to love how much power is packed into its slim design. Here are a few things to get you up and running.
 
iCloud helps keep you organized and up to date. You can safely store all your presentations, spreadsheets, PDFs, images, and other important files in iCloud with iCloud Drive. So you can access them on all your devices, whenever and wherever it’s convenient. iCloud also makes sure you always have the latest versions of documents, apps, notes, and contacts on all your devices. It even helps you find your iPad if you lose it. To get started, go to Settings, select
iCloud, and turn on the features you want.1
Set up iCloud
 
Whether it’s gaming, managing your finances, making movies, photography, traveling, working out, or anything else you’re into, there are hundreds of thousands of apps to help you do it better.2 And they’re all designed specifically for iPad. We’ve organized them by category in the App Store to make finding the perfect app fast and easy.
Learn more about apps for iPad
 
Millions of albums, a huge selection of movies and TV shows, and an amazing library of books are all waiting for your enjoyment.3
 
To share purchases from the iTunes Store, iBooks, and the App Store with up to six family members, simply go to Settings, tap iCloud, tap Set Up Family Sharing, then tap Get Started. Confirm that you want to be the family organizer and that you’re signed in with your personal Apple ID. Then just follow the onscreen instructions, and you’re ready to go.4
 
iCloud Photo Library lets you access, edit, and share your entire photo and video library from any
of your devices. Snap a selfie on your iPad and it’s automatically in the cloud. Make edits to your
videos and instantly see your updates on all your devices. All you have to do is go to Settings, tap
iCloud, tap Photos, then turn on iCloud Photo Sharing. Now all your photos will be organized the
same way across your devices. And they’re ready to be shared with friends too.
 
Continuity features ensure that your iPad and all your devices are always in sync and working together.5 For example, when you start an email on your iPad, an icon appears on the Lock screen of your iPhone. Swipe up and the email is there, waiting for you to continue. And, working in conjunction with your iPhone, your iPad can receive your phone calls. Just swipe the notification to answer, ignore, or respond with a quick message. You can even start browsing the web on your Mac and continue from the same link on your iPad. All you have to do is go to Settings, tap iCloud, and select whatever you want to share among devices.
 
With Touch ID, your iPad is more secure since your fingerprint is your passcode. It’s also easier to log in to secure apps and sites and make purchases within apps. Your iPad can even recognize the fingerprints of up to five others you want to grant access to. Getting started is easy. Just go to Settings, then tap Touch ID & Passcode.
 
Apple Pay makes online shopping easier than ever. To get started, you can add the credit or debit card from your iTunes account to Wallet by simply entering the card security code. To add a new card, use the iSight camera to capture the card information or enter your card number manually. When you’re ready to check out, select Apple Pay, touch your finger lightly on the Home button, and you’re done — no need to type in shipping and billing information.6
 
Once you sign up for a cellular plan, your iPad stays connected anywhere, even when you’re not on a Wi-Fi network. You can browse the web, send messages, stream movies and music, and make FaceTime calls from more places. Even better, plans are flexible with no long-term commitments. And you can use your iPad as a personal hotspot for your Mac or other devices.7 To set it up, go to Settings and select Cellular Data. Then select Set Up Cellular Data to find the carrier and plan that are right for you.
Learn more about cellular data and Wi-Fi
 
Check out the new, more colorful covers and cases designed just for iPad. And see other Apple-designed accessories for your photos, music, and more.
Learn more about iPad accessories
